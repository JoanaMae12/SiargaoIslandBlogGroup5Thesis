Siargao Island

The Ultimate Tropical Escape

Discover Southeast Asia's most sought-after tropical paradise, blending world-class surf breaks, turquoise lagoons, and laid-back island culture.

Quick Facts & Location

Location

Northeastern tip of Mindanao, Philippines

Region

Caraga

Capital

437 sq km

Population

~90,000 residents

Signature Attractions

Cloud 9 Surf Break

Why go? Renowned for its perfect peeling reef break, Cloud 9 hosts international surf competitions every year.

Level: Intermediate to pro-friendly.

Tip: Best waves from September to November.

Magpupungko Rock Pools

What to see: When the tide's low, crystal-clear tidal pools appear between jagged rock formations.

Experience: Swim, cliff-jump, and snap Insta-worthy shots.

Advice: Check tide charts before heading out.

Sugba Lagoon

Highlight: Emerald waters fringed by mangroves and limestone cliffs.

Activities: Paddleboarding, kayaking, and diving from the floating cottages.

Best time: Morning light for perfect reflections.

Island-Hopping Adventures

Popular stops: Guyam, Daku, and Naked Islands.

Don't miss: Beach BBQs on Daku Island, and the powder-white sands of Naked.

Duration: Half-day to full-day tours available.

Naked Island

Highlight: A bare stretch of dazzling white sand surrounded by clear blue waters.

Experience: Sunbathe, swim, and marvel at 360° island views.

Tip: No shade or vendors—bring water and sunscreen!

Palm Tree Road

Highlight: Iconic road lined with hundreds of swaying coconut trees.

Photo-op: Popular Instagram spot—go early to avoid traffic.

Nearby: Leads to Maasin River and Coconut View Deck.

Maasin River

Experience: Bamboo rafting, rope swing, and tranquil river views.

Cost: ₱20 entrance; extra for raft or guide.

Vibe: Lush, slow-paced, and perfect for relaxing exploration.

Coconut View Deck

Highlight: Sweeping views of Siargao’s endless coconut plantations.

Photo-op: Catch golden hour for the best shots.

Tip: Quick stop on the way to Magpupungko or Maasin.

Daku Island

Why visit: Lush island with palm trees, huts, and soft sand.

Must-try: Grilled seafood lunch served by locals.

Extras: Great for beach volleyball and swimming.

Best Time to Visit & Weather Tips

Siargao enjoys a tropical climate with two primary seasons:

Dry Season

March–October

Sunny days, ideal for surfing and beach activities.

Wet Season

November–February

Occasional rain showers; lush landscapes and fewer crowds.

Pro tip: Surf enthusiasts should plan for the "magic season" (September–October) when offshore winds and swell align for epic waves.

Getting There & Getting Around

How to Arrive

By Air

Daily flights to Sayak Airport (IAO) from Manila, Cebu, and Clark.

By Sea

Ferries from Surigao City connect to Dapa Port (2–3 hrs).

Moving Around

E-trikes

Electric tricycles are eco-friendly and cost ₱50–₱100 per ride.

Rentals

Scooters and motorbikes (₱400–₱600/day) for flexible island exploration.

Boats

Bangka tours for island-hopping and lagoon trips (₱1,500–₱2,500 per group).

Suggested 4-Day Itinerary (With Tipid Tips)

1

Arrival + Chill

Arrival: Via Sayak Airport or Dapa Port.
Stay: Tres Islas Hostel or Ilakai Hostel (₱350–₱400 dorm / ₱900–₱1,300 private).
Meals: Food stalls in General Luna (₱60–₱90).
Transport: Rent a motorbike instead of a taxi (₱300/day).
Sunset: Walk to Cloud 9 Boardwalk (Free).
Optional: Drinks with hostel friends (₱150).
2

Island Hopping / Inland Adventure

Option A - Island Hopping: Visit Naked Island, Daku Island, and Guyam Island (₱400–₱500/pax shared boat).
Environmental Fee: ₱100 | Lunch at Daku: ₱100–₱150.
Option B - Inland DIY (motorbike): Palm Tree Road (Free), Maasin River (₱20), Coconut View Deck (₱20), Magpupungko Rock Pools (₱50 + ₱20 parking).
Meals: Roadside eatery (~₱100), BBQ or hostel dinner (~₱80).
3

Surf, Markets, & Locals

Morning: Surf session at Cloud 9 (₱300 board rental).
Lesson: Optional instructor (negotiable rates).
Evening: Chill night at hostel — bonfire or movie.
Optional: Smoothie or beer (₱50–₱100).
Ref: Watch video.
4

Slow Travel & Departure

Sunrise: Ride to Secret Beach or Cemetery Beach (Free).
Breakfast: Local favorite — Tapsilog (₱70).
Souvenirs: Handmade bracelets or keychains (₱50–₱100).
Return: Head back to port or Sayak Airport.
Tip: Whole trip costs under ₱6,000 per person for 4 days!

Kalibu Brewery

RumBar

Sustainable Travel Pointers

Use reef-safe sunscreen

Protect coral reefs by choosing non-nano zinc or mineral-based SPF.

Hire local guides

Support community livelihoods and gain authentic insights.

Waste-free tips

Carry a reusable water bottle and say no to single-use plastics.

Eco-friendly tours

Opt for operators with environmental certifications and responsible practices.
